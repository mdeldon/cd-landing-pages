What Is an SSDI Hearing?

An ALJ (Administrative Law Judge) hearing is your strongest opportunity in the SSDI process. This is where most claims are actually approved — but only with the right preparation.

  • 1
    Your hearing is your moment to tell your story. Unlike paper reviews, you'll speak directly with a judge who will ask about your condition, your daily life, and what keeps you from working.
  • 2
    Preparation is everything. Studies show represented claimants are 3x more likely to win. A judge needs to see medical evidence, expert opinions, and a clear picture of your limitations.
  • 3
    We've been there thousands of times. Citizens Disability has helped 50,000+ clients win at hearing. We know what judges look for — and how to present your case effectively.
